Pollicino
 Había una vez un pobre campesino que una noche estaba sentado junto al fuego, mientras su esposa hilaba.
En un momento dado, dijo: "¡Qué triste es no tener hijos! Nuestra casa está tan silenciosa mientras en las otras hay tanto bullicio y alegría".
"Sí" respondió la mujer suspirando "aunque fuera uno solo, y pequeñito, no más grande que un pulgar, ya estaría contenta, y le querremos mucho."
Entonces la mujer empezó a sentirse mal, y después de siete meses dio a luz a un niño, perfectamente formado, pero no más grande que un pulgar.
Entonces dijeron: "Es justo lo que deseábamos, y será nuestro querido hijito"; y, por su estatura, lo llamaron Pollito.
No le escatimaron la comida, sin embargo, el niño no creció y permaneció como al momento del nacimiento. Tenía una mirada inteligente y pronto se mostró como un pequeño atento y juicioso, que lograba todo lo que emprendía.
Un día, el campesino se preparaba para ir al bosque a cortar leña, y murmuró: "¡Si hubiera alguien que viniera a recogerme con el carro!".
"Oh papá" exclamó Pollito "lo haré yo; el carro estará en el bosque a su debido tiempo."
El hombre se puso a reír y dijo: "¿Cómo es posible? Eres demasiado pequeño para guiar un caballo con las riendas".
"No importa, papá, si mamá quiere atarlo, me meteré en el oído del caballo y le diré dónde debe ir"
"Be'" respondió el contadino "probemos, por esta vez."
Cuando llegó la hora, la madre ató y puso a Pollito en el oído del caballo, y el pequeño le gritaba dónde debía ir: "¡Uh y oh! ¡a la derecha y a la izquierda!".
Todo ocurrió como si hubiera habido un cochero, y el carro se dirigía recto hacia el bosque. Entonces, en una curva, mientras el niño gritaba: "¡A la izquierda!", pasaron dos extranjeros por allí.
"¡Dios mío!" dijo uno "¡qué es esto? ¡Hay un carro y un cochero invisible guiando al caballo!" "Algo no está bien" dijo el otro "sígámoslo y veamos dónde se detiene."
Mientras tanto, el carro se adentraba en el bosque donde estaban cortando leña. Cuando Pollito vio a su padre, gritó: "¡Viste, papá, aquí estoy con el carro, ahora sácame!"
El campesino agarró al caballo con la izquierda, y con la derecha tiró de la oreja de su hijito, que muy contento se sentó sobre una brizna de paja.
Cuando los dos extranjeros vieron a Pollito, se quedaron mudos de asombro, uno tomó al otro aparte y dijo: "Escucha, ese hombrecito podría hacernos ricos, si lo mostramos por dinero en una gran ciudad: ¡vamos a comprarlo!".

They approached the farmer and said, "Sell us the little man, we'll treat him well!". - "No," replied the father, "I wouldn't sell the root of my heart for all the gold in the world.". But Tom Thumb, hearing the deal, climbed up the folds of the man's clothes, sat on his shoulder, and whispered in his ear, "Daddy, go ahead and sell me, I'll come back to you." So the father gave him to those two for a beautiful gold coin. "Where do you want to put yourself?" they asked him. "Ah, put me on the brim of the hat; there I can go up and down like in a gallery, and admire the landscape." They complied, and when Tom Thumb had said goodbye to his father, they took him away. They walked until dusk, then the little one said, "Pull me down, I need it." - "You can stay there," replied the man who carried him on his hat, "I don't mind; even birds drop something occasionally." - "No," said Tom Thumb, "I know what's proper; pull me down, quickly!" The man took off his hat and put the little one in a field along the road; and he went a little way among the clods here and there; then suddenly he slipped into a mouse hole, which he had deliberately sought. "Good evening, gentlemen, you can go without me!" he shouted to them. They ran and poked the hole with sticks, but it was wasted effort. Tom Thumb kept sliding down, and since soon it was pitch dark, they had to leave full of anger and with empty pockets. When Tom Thumb realized they were gone, he came out of the underground gallery. "It's dangerous to walk through the fields in the dark," he said, "it's so easy to break one's neck!" Luckily he came across a snail shell: "Praise be to God!" he thought. "Here I can safely spend the night!" and he went in. Shortly after, as he was about to fall asleep, he heard two men passing by, one of whom said, "How will we steal the gold and silver from the rich parish priest?" "I could tell you," Tom Thumb immediately shouted. "What was that?" exclaimed one of the thieves, startled. "I heard someone talking." They stopped to listen, and Tom Thumb said again, "Take me with you, I'll help you." - "Where are you?" - "Look on the ground and listen to where the voice is coming from," he replied. Finally, the thieves found him and lifted him up. "But how do you want to help us, little worm!" they said. "Look," he replied, "I'll enter through the bars into the priest's room and hand you out what you want." - "Well," they said, "we'll see what you're capable of." When they arrived at the rectory, Tom Thumb entered the room, but immediately shouted at the top of his lungs, "Do you want everything that's here?" The thieves, frightened, said, "Speak softly, so no one hears you!" But Tom Thumb pretended not to understand and shouted again, "What do you want? Do you want everything that's here?" The cook who was sleeping in the next room heard him and sat up in bed listening. But because of the fright, the thieves had run behind a piece; finally, they regained their courage and thought, "That little fellow wants to make fools of us." They returned and whispered to him, "Now do it for real and give us something." And again Tom Thumb shouted as loud as he could, "I'll give you everything; just reach out your hands!" The woman listening heard him distinctly, jumped out of bed, and stumbled into the room. The thieves hurriedly fled, as if they had fire at their heels; but the woman, unable to see anything, went to light a lamp. When she returned, Tom Thumb, unseen, slipped into the barn; and the woman, after searching in vain in all corners, went back to bed, thinking she had been daydreaming.

Pollicino si era arrampicato fra gli steli del fieno, dove aveva trovato un bel posto per dormire: voleva riposare fino a giorno per poi fare ritorno dai genitori. Ma lo aspettavano ben altre esperienze! Sì, non mancano tribolazioni e affanni a questo mondo! All'alba la serva si alzò per dar da mangiare alle bestie. Per prima cosa andò nel fienile, dove prese una manciata di fieno, proprio quello in cui dormiva il povero Pollicino. Ma egli dormiva così sodo che non se ne accorse e si svegliò soltanto in bocca alla mucca, che l'aveva preso con il fieno. -Ah, Dio mio!- gridò. -Come ho fatto a cadere nella mangiatoia?- Ma capì ben presto dove si trovava. Alla fine, impaurito, gridò con quanto fiato aveva in gola: -Non portatemi più fieno! Non portatemi più fieno!-. Ma ci aveva appena messo piede, che Pollicino si mise a gridare di nuovo: -Non portatemi più fieno! Non portatemi più fieno!-. Allora anche il parroco si spaventò e, pensando che si trattasse di uno spirito maligno, fece uccidere la mucca. Così fu macellata, ma lo stomaco dove si trovava Pollicino fu gettato nel letamaio. Pollicino cercò di tirarsi fuori con gran fatica; finalmente riuscì a farsi strada, ma proprio mentre stava per metter fuori la testa sopraggiunse un'altra disgrazia. Arrivò di corsa un lupo affamato che ingoiò tutto lo stomaco in un boccone. Ma Pollicino non si perse d'animo. Il lupo non se lo fece dire due volte: di notte, passando dalla cantina, entrò nella dispensa e mangiò a sazietà.
